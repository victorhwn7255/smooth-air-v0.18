# 01 — Project: what SmoothAir is

**Snapshot: 2026-06-15.**

## One paragraph

SmoothAir answers one anxious question — **"Will my flight be bumpy?"** A
nervous flyer types a flight number; the app returns an honest smoothness
**grade**, a **flight ribbon** of turbulence zones along the flight timeline,
per-zone **detail cards** (where / when / how likely / why / how confident), a
plain-language **briefing paragraph** ("what to tell the cabin"), and a
collapsible route table for the curious. After landing, a one-tap "How was it?"
records reality. It is a **comfort tool, not a flight-planning tool**.

## Who it's for

A single family — people on iPhones who may be **anxious about flying** and may
be **older / non-technical**. Design and copy consequence: **clarity beats
cleverness, every time.** Calm, plain language. No jargon in the user's face.

## The mission (the emotional job)

Replace *dread of the unknown* with *calm, specific, honest expectations*. The
user should come away knowing what to expect, when, and how much to trust it —
including when the honest answer is "we don't really know."

## Honesty principles — BINDING (these are the soul of the product)

Every screen must obey these. They are not style preferences; violating them
breaks the product's reason to exist.

1. **Grades, not fake-precise scores.** "Mostly smooth — one bumpy stretch",
   never "73.4/100".
2. **Probability bands in prose, not bare decimals.** "Roughly 1-in-3 chance of
   bumps." A raw percentage may appear only as secondary detail, never as the
   headline number.
3. **Confidence is always visible.** Every briefing shows how much to trust it
   (high / medium / low), driven by forecast lead time. Never hide uncertainty.
4. **Failures are loud and honest.** If live weather is unavailable, show a
   clear **DEMO DATA** banner — never silently present stale or fake data as
   real. If a flight is too far out (>5 days), refuse with a plain reason.
5. **The disclaimer is always reachable** — "comfort briefing only, not for
   flight planning; crews see more and steer around the worst."

## What success looks like

A scared family member taps a flight (or opens a shared link), and in ~2
seconds gets a calm, specific, honestly-hedged read on their flight that they
trust — because the app is visibly honest about what it does and doesn't know.
